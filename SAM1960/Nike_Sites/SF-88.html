Nike Missile Site SF-88

Marin County, California

Construction of SF-88 was under way by July 7, 1954, the Launch Site belongs to Fort Barry and the Control Site belongs to Fort Cronkhite, in the Marin Headlands to the north of San Francisco. The site was originally armed with Nike Ajax missiles, and modifications were made to the site in 1958 to allow it to also be armed with Nike Hercules missiles. In 1974, SF-88 was closed but was not demolished. It is now part of the Golden Gate National Recreation Area and is open to visitors.

The SF-88 Launch Site is the most fully restored Nike missile launch site in the country, now operating by the National Park Service. Visiting day is Thursday, Friday, and Saturday from 12:00 - 3:00 PM, with formal tours occurring every 45 minutes. A special open house day with Nike veterans occurs on the first Saturday of each month. Restoration work to the site is done on remaining Saturdays by volunteers.

The SF-88 Control Site is also a part of the Golden Gate National Recreation Area, it locates at the peak of Hill 88, but it's not operating by any authority or organization. Visitors could reach the abolished site by the Coastal Trail.
