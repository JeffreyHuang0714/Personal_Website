Fantasy RPG Map Tileset

Mingrui Huang

I am a big fan of fantasy RPGs like The Witcher and The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim. This map tileset gets inspired by these fantasy RPGs' in-game maps. It uses a brown-grey color design to simulate the texture of old parchment.

In the map, castles represent capitals, outposts represent major cities, other cities and palces are only labeled by text.

This map tileset has only few details and information showing on the map, the visual form is relatively simple. Thus, it is a deactivated and pleasant map on the dimension of visual form.

This tileset uses relatively dark color hues, and most of them have low contrast between each other. Considering that darker color usually has a negative meaning in western culture, this tileset is activated and unpleasant in the dimension of color.

I used a font family called “Morpheus” for this map tileset, it looks just like the font used in The Witcher. It’s a very exaggerated serif type and has sharp edges, which is very complicated. So, the map tileset is activated and unpleasant in the dimension of type.

All in all, this map tileset is the one relatively activated and unpleasant due to the color and type used.
